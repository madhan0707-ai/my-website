UK visas for international students

To study at Lancaster University International Study Centre, you may need a student visa. With 99% success, our team supports thousands of international students each year in their applications.

Secure your visa in 6 simple steps

1. Accept your offer

Confirm your place, then meet any conditions of your offer (if applicable).

2. Complete the CAS data check

And any required credibility tasks. Once submitted, you should receive your CAS in 1 working day.

3. Apply and pay for your visa online

You can apply up to 6 months before your course start date, once you have your CAS.

4. Pay the Immigration Health Surcharge

This grants you access to the UK’s National Health Service (NHS).

5. Attend a biometrics appointment

Submit the required supporting documents and confirm your identity.

6. Wait for a decision

This can take 1–4 weeks on average depending on the service you choose.

Frequently asked questions

Most international students need a Student visa to study in the UK. Whether you need one depends on your nationality and the length of your course.

You apply online through the UK government website once you have received your CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) from us.

You can apply for your visa up to 6 months before your course start date, once you have received your CAS.

You will need to show proof of funds to cover your tuition fees and living costs, as required by the UK Home Office's financial requirements.

You will typically need your passport, CAS, proof of finances, and any required English language or academic qualification certificates.

An eVisa is an online record of your immigration status in the UK, replacing physical visa stickers and biometric residence permits.

Working rights depend on your visa conditions. Your visa documentation will confirm whether and how many hours you are permitted to work.

Our support team is available to guide you through every step of your visa application, from document checks to interview preparation.
